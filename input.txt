1.  Project Overview
This document is the full website development specification for Global Shore Fin Services Inc. It covers every requirement the developer needs: site purpose, page structure, service categories, content guidelines, UI design system, UX flows, blog strategy, SEO, and technical stack. All decisions should be made in line with this document before any design or development begins.

1.1  Company Background
Global Shore Fin Services Inc. is a full-service financial outsourcing company headquartered in Tennessee State US, serving US-based individuals and businesses. The company offers US Payroll, US Payroll taxation, US Sales and use Tax, Individual Taxation, Business Taxation, US accounting, compliance, and business formation services. The long-term vision is to expand coverage to India, Canada, and Australia, but Phase 1 of the website focuses exclusively on the US market.

1.2  Reference Websites Studied
The following two websites were reviewed as design and content references:
•	globalsaitaxfilers.com — clean service-first layout, strong CTA placement, good use of hero sections and service cards.
•	taxmonkglobal.com — professional global branding, clear service categories, credibility-building with testimonials and stats.
The Global Shore website should match this standard in professionalism, exceed it in clarity, and be built to scale globally as new markets are added.

Goal Statement
Build a professional, fast-loading, SEO-optimised website that positions Global Shore Fin Services Inc. as a trusted US financial services partner for Indian professionals, NRIs, small businesses, and immigrants — while laying the foundation for a global multi-country expansion.

3.  Service Descriptions — What to Write on Each Page
Every service page must follow this content structure: (1) Hero headline with the service name and a one-line benefit statement. (2) What this service is — a short paragraph in plain English. (3) Who it is for — bullet list of target clients. (4) What is included — bullet list of deliverables. (5) Why choose Global Shore — 2–3 differentiators. (6) FAQ — 3 to 5 common questions with answers. (7) CTA — 'Book a Free Consultation' button.

Payroll Services
Description: End-to-end payroll processing for US businesses. We calculate employee wages, deduct federal and state taxes, issue pay stubs, manage direct deposits, and file quarterly payroll reports.
Who it's for: Small businesses, startups, mid-size companies with W-2 employees, businesses with multi-state payroll needs.
Includes: Weekly / bi-weekly / monthly payroll runs, W-2 / 1099 generation, direct deposit setup, payroll registers, year-end reporting.

Payroll Taxation Services
Description: Accurate calculation and remittance of all payroll-related taxes including federal income tax withholding, FICA (Social Security & Medicare), FUTA/SUTA, and state income taxes.
Includes: Federal 941/940 filings, state unemployment insurance, payroll tax deposits, penalty avoidance advisory.

Payroll Compliance
Description: Ensuring your payroll practices comply with US federal and state labour laws, DOL requirements, and IRS regulations.
Includes: Compliance audits, worker classification (W-2 vs 1099), minimum wage compliance, record retention guidance.

Bookkeeping Services
Description: Organised, accurate recording of all financial transactions. We maintain your general ledger, categorise income and expenses, and produce monthly reports.
Includes: Monthly transaction recording, chart of accounts setup, income statements, balance sheets, QuickBooks/Xero management.

Accounting Services
Description: Full-cycle accounting support including financial statement preparation, management reporting, budgeting, and strategic financial analysis.
Includes: Monthly/quarterly/annual financial statements, cash flow analysis, budget vs actual reporting, CFO advisory.

Bank Reconciliations
Description: Monthly matching of your bank statements against your books to catch errors, fraud, and discrepancies.
Includes: Monthly reconciliation reports, variance explanations, journal entry corrections.

Cash Reconciliations
Description: Reconciliation of cash balances across multiple accounts, petty cash, and POS systems to ensure accuracy.

Accounts Receivable
Description: Management of all money owed to your business — from invoicing through collection follow-up.
Includes: Invoice generation, AR ageing reports, payment follow-up, bad debt tracking.

Accounts Payable
Description: Management of all bills and vendor payments to keep your business in good standing and optimise cash flow.
Includes: Vendor invoice entry, payment scheduling, AP ageing reports, 1099 vendor tracking.

Individual Taxation
Description: Federal and state income tax return preparation for US residents, NRIs, green card holders, H1B / L1 visa holders, and US citizens abroad.
Includes: Form 1040, state returns, FBAR/FATCA filings, ITIN applications, amended returns (1040-X), tax planning.

Business Taxation
Description: Tax preparation and planning for all US business entity types.
Includes: Form 1120 (C-Corp), 1120-S (S-Corp), 1065 (Partnership), 1040 Schedule C (Sole Proprietor), estimated quarterly taxes, state business returns.

Expat Taxation
Description: Specialised tax services for US citizens and green card holders living outside the US, and for foreign nationals working in the US.
Includes: Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (Form 2555), Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116), FBAR (FinCEN 114), Form 8938, tax treaty analysis.

Sales & Use Tax Services
Description: Registration, filing, and compliance management for sales tax across all US states where your business has nexus.
Includes: Nexus analysis, state registration, monthly/quarterly/annual filings, VDA (Voluntary Disclosure Agreements).

Auditing
Description: Independent review and verification of financial records to ensure accuracy and compliance.
Includes: Internal audit support, preparation for external audits, audit readiness reviews, financial statement audits for small businesses.

LLC Formation & Company Incorporation
Description: Complete business formation service for individuals and NRIs looking to register a business in the US.
Includes: State selection advisory, Articles of Organisation / Incorporation filing, EIN application, operating agreement drafting, registered agent service, S-Corp election, banking setup guidance.

Other State Compliance
Description: Ongoing compliance filings for businesses operating in multiple US states.
Includes: Annual reports, foreign qualification filings, state business licence renewals, registered agent maintenance.

6.  UI Design System

6.1  Brand Colours
Colour Name	Hex Value & Usage
Primary Navy	#0D3B6E — used for headings, header background, CTA fills
Primary Teal	#0F6E56 — used for accents, hover states, section highlights
Light Background	#F2F4F7 — alternating section backgrounds
White	#FFFFFF — card backgrounds, form fields
Dark Text	#101828 — all body copy
Muted Text	#667085 — captions, metadata, helper text
Warning / Accent	#F79009 — used sparingly for badges, highlights

6.2  Typography
•	Heading Font: Inter (Google Fonts) — Bold (700) for H1/H2, Semibold (600) for H3.
•	Body Font: Inter — Regular (400) for body copy, 16px minimum size.
•	Fallback stack: 'Inter', 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif.
•	No decorative or script fonts anywhere on the site.
•	Minimum font size on mobile: 15px.

6.3  Typography Scale
Element	Specification
H1 — Page Title	48px desktop / 32px mobile, Bold, Navy
H2 — Section Heading	36px desktop / 26px mobile, Bold, Navy
H3 — Sub-Heading	24px desktop / 20px mobile, Semibold, Teal
Body / Paragraph	16px, Regular, #101828, line-height 1.7
Caption / Label	13px, Regular, #667085
CTA Button Text	15px, Semibold, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.5px

6.4  Buttons
•	Primary Button: Navy fill (#0D3B6E), white text, 8px border-radius, 48px height, hover darkens 10%.
•	Secondary Button: Teal outline, teal text, no fill, hover fills teal with white text.
•	Ghost Button: No fill, no border, underline only — used inside cards for 'Learn More' links.
•	Floating WhatsApp Button: Green (#25D366), fixed bottom-right, 56px circle, icon only.

6.5  Cards & Components
•	Service Cards: white background, 1px border (#E4E7EC), 12px radius, 24px padding, hover lifts with subtle shadow.
•	Blog Cards: thumbnail (16:9), tag badge, title (H3 weight), excerpt text, 'Read More' link.
•	Stat Boxes: dark navy background, large number (48px bold white), label below (14px muted).
•	Testimonial Cards: quote icon, text, client name, location, service tag, star rating.
•	Team Cards: circular photo (120×120), name, designation, LinkedIn icon.

6.6  Spacing System
•	Base unit: 8px. All spacing should be multiples of 8 (8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96px).
•	Section vertical padding: 96px top and bottom on desktop, 64px on mobile.
•	Container max-width: 1200px, centred.
•	Grid gap: 24px default.

6.7  Icons
•	Use Lucide Icons or Heroicons — clean, line-based, consistent stroke weight.
•	Icon size in cards: 40×40px in a 72×72 teal-tinted circle background.
•	Do not use flat-colour emoji or inconsistent icon libraries.

6.8  Imagery
•	No generic stock photos of people pointing at charts or shaking hands.
•	Use abstract finance illustrations (isometric style preferred), city skylines, or geometric patterns.
•	Source: unDraw.co, Storyset, Freepik premium (with license), or commission custom illustrations.
•	All images must be compressed (WebP format, max 150KB per image).


7.  UX — User Experience Specifications

7.1  Primary User Journeys to Optimise
Journey 1 — NRI / Immigrant Tax Help
•	Lands on Home → reads 'Who We Serve' → clicks 'NRI / Immigrant' → reads service page → books consultation.

Journey 2 — Small Business Looking for Payroll
•	Searches 'payroll services for small business' → lands on Payroll Services page → reads inclusions → fills contact form.

Journey 3 — Expat Needing Tax Filing
•	Lands on Expat Taxation page from Google → reads FAQs → clicks WhatsApp button → begins conversation.

7.2  Navigation UX
•	Sticky header on scroll — logo left, nav centre, 'Book Consultation' CTA button right.
•	Services dropdown: mega-menu with all 5 categories and their sub-services listed.
•	Mobile: hamburger menu, full-screen slide-in panel, accordion for service categories.
•	Breadcrumbs on all inner pages for navigation and SEO.

7.3  Conversion Optimisation
•	Every service page must have at least two CTAs: one above the fold and one at the bottom.
•	Exit-intent popup: 'Before you go — get a free tax checklist.' Collects email.
•	Free consultation offer must appear on: home hero, about page, every service page, contact page.
•	WhatsApp button visible on all pages at all times (floating).
•	Calendly or similar booking widget embedded on the Contact page (removes friction).

7.4  Accessibility
•	WCAG 2.1 AA compliant minimum.
•	All images must have descriptive alt text.
•	All form fields must have labels (not just placeholder text).
•	Colour contrast ratio minimum 4.5:1 for body text.
•	Keyboard navigable: all interactive elements reachable and operable via keyboard.

7.5  Mobile UX
•	Mobile-first design — design for 375px screen width first, then scale up.
•	Tap targets minimum 44×44px.
•	No horizontal scrolling on any page.
•	Click-to-call and click-to-WhatsApp links on all contact references.
•	Forms: large inputs, numeric keyboards for phone fields, auto-fill enabled.

7.6  Page Speed & Performance
•	Target: Google PageSpeed score 90+ on mobile and desktop.
•	Lazy-load all images below the fold.
•	No heavy JavaScript libraries unless absolutely necessary.
•	Use a CDN for all static assets.
•	Core Web Vitals targets: LCP < 2.5s, FID < 100ms, CLS < 0.1.

9.  SEO & Technical Requirements

9.1  On-Page SEO
•	Every page must have a unique title tag (50–60 characters) and meta description (150–160 characters).
•	H1 tag must appear exactly once per page and contain the primary keyword.
•	URL structure: /services/payroll-services/, /blog/h1b-tax-filing-guide/ — lowercase, hyphens only.
•	Schema markup: Organisation, LocalBusiness, FAQPage on service pages, BlogPosting on blog posts.
•	XML sitemap and robots.txt must be configured at launch.

9.2  Target Keywords (Primary)

9.3  Technical SEO

•	Canonical tags on all pages to avoid duplicate content.

11.  Content Tone & Voice Guidelines

All written content on the website must follow these tone guidelines so the copy feels consistent whether written by the company, a copywriter, or a content agency.

•	Professional but approachable — not cold or corporate. Think 'trusted advisor' not 'big accounting firm'.
•	Plain English — no jargon unless necessary. When a tax term is used, explain it simply.
•	Empathetic — many clients are NRIs, immigrants, or first-time US business owners who find US taxes confusing. Acknowledge that and reassure.
•	Action-oriented — every page should guide the reader toward a next step (read this, contact us, download this).
•	Confident but not arrogant — show expertise, not ego.
•	Global but US-focused — for Phase 1, all examples and references should be US-specific.
